⚠ 4-pin tactile buttons — common mistake:
The two legs on the same side of the button body are always shorted together internally. Wiring both wires to that side permanently connects the GPIO to GND — the button appears "always pressed" and nothing works.

Rule: the two wires must go to legs on opposite sides of the button.

Test with a multimeter (continuity mode):
· Beeps without pressing → same side → wrong
· Only beeps when pressed → correct pair ✓

Most KY-040 encoders run at 3.3 V and connect directly to Pi GPIO. If your module has a separate + and GND on the board, use Pi pin 17 (3.3 V) and any GND pin. CLK/DT/SW go directly to the GPIO pins — no resistors needed.

I²C Bus — DS3231 RTC & BH1750 Light Sensor

Both chips share the same two wires (SDA + SCL) — wire them in parallel. The Pi addresses each by its unique address: DS3231 = 0x68, BH1750 = 0x23.

Do not add external pull-up resistors — GPIO 2 & 3 have hardware pull-ups built into the Pi 4.
